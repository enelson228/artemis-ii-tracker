Mission Overview

Artemis II is the first crewed mission beyond low Earth orbit since Apollo 17 in 1972. It will test the Space Launch System rocket and Orion spacecraft with a four-person crew on a lunar free-return trajectory, paving the way for future lunar surface missions.

Launch Vehicle Space Launch System (SLS)
Spacecraft Orion Capsule
Crew Reid Wiseman, Victor Glover, Christina Koch, Jeremy Hansen
Mission Duration ~10 Days
Recovery Pacific Ocean (U.S. Navy)

Flight Path

Free return trajectory around the Moon.

1 LAUNCH

Astronauts lift off from Kennedy Space Center.

2 JETTISON

Solid Rocket Boosters and Launch Abort System separated.

3 MECO

Core stage main engine cut off.

4 PERIGEE RAISE

First maneuver to elevate initial orbit.

5 APOGEE BURN

Push to High Earth Orbit (HEO) for 23.5-hr checkout.

6 SEPARATION

Orion separates from ICPS.

7 USS BURN

Upper Stage Separation validation.

8 PERIGEE RAISE

Burn to shape the trajectory for lunar injection.

9 TLI BURN

Trans-Lunar Injection starts the free return journey.

10 OUTBOUND

Travel to Moon takes approx 4 days.

11 LUNAR FLYBY

Reaching ~4,047 miles beyond lunar far side.

12 RETURN

Gravity-assisted Trans-Earth trajectory.

13 CM SEP

Crew module separates from service module.

14 ENTRY

Earth's atmosphere interface.

15 SPLASHDOWN

Recovery of astronauts and capsule.
